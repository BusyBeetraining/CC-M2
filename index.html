Mojo Dialer: Complete Setup Guide

Mojo is the most popular dialer for wholesaling cold callers. Here's everything you need to know:

Login and Initial Setup

1 Access Your Account

Your supervisor will provide login credentials. Go to mojosells.com and click "Sign In"

2 Download the Desktop App

Mojo works best as a desktop application. Download for Windows or Mac. The web version exists but is limited.

3 Configure Your Microphone

Go to Settings ‚Üí Audio. Test your microphone and headset. Adjust volume levels. Crystal-clear audio is critical.

4 Set Your Caller ID

Your supervisor will assign you a local caller ID number. This increases answer rates because it shows as a local call.

Importing and Managing Lists

1 Import Your List

Click "Lists" ‚Üí "Import List" ‚Üí Upload CSV file with columns: First Name, Last Name, Phone, Address, Property Info

2 DNC Scrubbing (CRITICAL)

Before calling ANY list, run DNC scrubbing. Mojo automatically removes numbers on the National Do Not Call Registry. Skipping this step = legal violations.

3 Select Your Calling List

Click "Select List" ‚Üí Choose your list ‚Üí Click "Start Dialing"

Triple-Line Dialing Explained

üî• Mojo's Secret Weapon: Triple-Line Dialing

Mojo calls 3 phone numbers simultaneously. Whoever answers first gets connected to you. The other 2 calls disconnect automatically. This triples your contact rate compared to single-line dialers.

Example: Instead of calling 20 numbers in an hour and reaching 6 people, you call 60 numbers and reach 18 people in the same hour.

Using Disposition Codes in Mojo

After EVERY call, you must mark a disposition code. This tracks your results and tells Mojo how to handle this lead next time.

C
Contact

You reached a live person but they're not interested and don't want a callback.

L
Lead

They expressed interest! Motivated seller, wants more info, or considering selling.

A
Appointment

You set an appointment for your wholesaler to visit the property!

CB
Callback

They want you to call back at a specific time. Schedule the callback.

NI
Not Interested

They're not selling and don't want to be contacted again.

WN
Wrong Number

Number is disconnected, wrong person, or bad data.

DNC
Do Not Call

They specifically requested to be removed from your list. Mark DNC immediately.

TCPA Compliance: Non-Negotiable Rules

üö® CRITICAL: TCPA Violations = Lawsuits

The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) regulates cold calling. Violations can result in $500-$1,500 fines PER CALL. Your wholesaler can be sued. You can be held personally liable. Take compliance seriously.

TCPA Compliance Checklist

ALWAYS scrub against National DNC Registry before calling

Every dialer has this feature. Use it. No exceptions.

Maintain your own company DNC list

If someone says "take me off your list," mark them DNC immediately and never call them again.

Only call between 8 AM - 9 PM in prospect's local time zone

Calling outside these hours is a TCPA violation. Set your dialer to respect time zones.

Don't call cell phones with auto-dialers without prior consent

Technically, auto-dialing cell phones requires permission. Most wholesalers accept this risk, but know the law.

Identify yourself and your company at the start of every call

"Hi, this is [Your Name] with [Company Name]..." Required by law.

Honor DNC requests immediately

When someone says "don't call me again," mark it in the system before you hang up.

Don't use pre-recorded messages without consent

Robocalls to cell phones = major TCPA violation. You must speak live.

DNC List Management

1 Before Calling: National DNC Scrub

All dialers integrate with the National DNC Registry. Scrub your list before every campaign. This removes people who registered on donotcall.gov

2 During Calls: Company DNC List

When someone says "take me off your list" or "don't call me again," mark them DNC in your dialer immediately.

3 Weekly: Export and Backup Your DNC List

Export your company DNC list weekly. Share with your team. Ensure no one accidentally calls these numbers.

‚ö†Ô∏è DNC Liability Example

A real estate investor called someone on the National DNC Registry 17 times over 6 months. The homeowner sued and won $25,500 in damages ($1,500 per call). The investor also paid legal fees. Don't let this be you.

Disposition Code Mastery

Every dialer uses disposition codes. Learn what each means and when to use them:

CONTACT (C)
When to Use:

You spoke with someone, confirmed it's the property owner, but they're not interested in selling right now and don't want a callback.

Next Action: Remove from active list

LEAD (L)
When to Use:

Property owner showed interest in selling, asked questions, or is considering it. This is a MOTIVATED SELLER!

Next Action: Add to hot leads list, follow up within 24-48 hours

APPOINTMENT (A)
When to Use:

You successfully scheduled your wholesaler to visit the property for an in-person assessment!

Next Action: Confirm appointment 24 hours before, send details to wholesaler

CALLBACK (CB)
When to Use:

Property owner wants you to call back at a specific time. They're busy now or need to think about it.

Next Action: Schedule exact callback date/time in system

NOT INTERESTED (NI)
When to Use:

Property owner clearly stated they're not selling and not interested. Polite but firm "no."

Next Action: Remove from active list, possibly try again in 6-12 months

WRONG NUMBER (WN)
When to Use:

Number is disconnected, belongs to wrong person, or bad data in your list.

Next Action: Mark as bad data, skip trace for updated contact info

DO NOT CALL (DNC)
When to Use:

Property owner explicitly said "take me off your list," "don't call me again," or "I'm on the DNC list."

Next Action: IMMEDIATELY mark DNC, never call again

NO ANSWER (NA)
When to Use:

Call went to voicemail or no one answered. Not technically a "disposition" but tracked for follow-up.

Next Action: Try again at different time, leave voicemail on 2nd attempt

Warm Transfer Process

When you reach a highly motivated seller who wants to talk RIGHT NOW, you can transfer them directly to your wholesaler:

1 Recognize the Hot Lead

Signals: "I need to sell this week," "I'll take any offer," "Can someone come look at it today?"

2 Ask Permission

"I have my property specialist available right now. Would you like to speak with them directly to discuss your situation?"

3 Warm Them Up

"Great! Let me tell them about your situation. Give me one moment."

4 Place on Hold (in dialer)

Most dialers have a "Hold" button. Click it. The prospect hears hold music.

5 Brief Your Wholesaler

Call or text: "Hot lead on line 1. John Smith, 123 Oak St. Inherited property, needs to sell ASAP, very motivated."

6 Transfer the Call

In Mojo: Click "Transfer" ‚Üí Enter wholesaler's extension ‚Üí "Warm Transfer"
In PhoneBurner: Click "Transfer" ‚Üí Select contact ‚Üí "Transfer Now"

üî• Warm vs. Cold Transfer

Warm Transfer: You brief the wholesaler first, then connect. (PREFERRED)

Cold Transfer: You transfer directly without briefing. (Use only if wholesaler is immediately available and prefers this)

Call Recording and Review

Recording your calls is one of the fastest ways to improve. Here's how to use this feature effectively:

1 Enable Recording in Your Dialer

All major dialers have this. Turn it on. Some states require disclosure ("This call may be recorded...").

2 Weekly Review Session

Every Friday, listen to 5-10 of your calls. Pick 2 where you got a lead, 2 where you got hung up on, and 1 random call.

3 Self-Critique

Ask yourself:

  • Did I talk too fast or too slow?
  • Did I listen or just pitch?
  • Did I miss buying signals?
  • How was my tone? Confident? Nervous?
  • Did I ask enough qualifying questions?

4 Share with Your Supervisor

Send your best call and worst call each week. Ask for feedback. Top performers do this religiously.

Common Dialer Problems & Solutions

Problem: Low Contact Rate (below 25%)

Solutions:

  • Call at different times (try mornings vs afternoons)
  • Your list quality is poor - get better data
  • Too many disconnected numbers - need fresh list
Problem: Audio Quality Issues

Solutions:

  • Use wired headset instead of Bluetooth
  • Close other applications using bandwidth
  • Test your internet speed (need 5+ Mbps)
  • Switch to different USB port for headset
Problem: Dialer Keeps Dropping Calls

Solutions:

  • Check internet connection stability
  • Restart the dialer application
  • Contact support - may be server issue
  • Clear cache and cookies if using web-based dialer
Problem: Can't Import List

Solutions:

  • Verify CSV format matches dialer requirements
  • Check column headers match exactly
  • Remove special characters from data
  • Ensure file size is under dialer limit
